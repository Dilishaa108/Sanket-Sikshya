Every dot tells
a story.

Braille uses a 6-dot grid — raised or flat — to represent every letter, number, and punctuation mark. Our interactive trainer helps you learn each pattern visually before feeling it in real life.

Did you know?

Braille was invented by Louis Braille in 1824 when he was just 15 years old — blind himself, he created the system that helps millions read with their fingertips.
