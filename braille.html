Each Braille cell has 6 dot positions arranged in 2 columns and 3 rows. Dots are numbered 1–6 from top to bottom, left column first.

Raised dots (glowing blue) are the ones you feel with your fingertips. Empty positions (dark) are flat. The combination of raised dots forms each letter.
