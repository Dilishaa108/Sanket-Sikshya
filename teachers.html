10-Week Classroom Curriculum

Free · Printable · Nepali Focused
Week 1–2
Foundation & Introduction
  • Introduce Braille cell concept — 6 dots
  • Learn letters A–E in Braille
  • Introduce NSL fingerspelling A–E
  • Daily 5-min warm-up routine
Week 3–4
Building Vocabulary
  • Braille letters F–J with dot patterns
  • NSL letters F–J hand shapes
  • First classroom game: Dot Bingo
  • Partner practice activities
Week 5–6
Midpoint Review
  • Braille letters K–O
  • NSL letters K–O
  • Mid-course quiz and celebration
  • Introduce fingerspelling short words
Week 7–8
Advanced Letters
  • Braille letters P–T
  • NSL letters P–T including motion letters J and Z
  • Spell your name in sign language
  • Group relay game activity
Week 9
Completion
  • Braille and NSL letters U–Z
  • Full alphabet review both systems
  • Student showcase and presentations
  • Peer teaching activity
Week 10
Celebration & Assessment
  • Final quiz — both Braille and Sign Language
  • Certificate of completion
  • Class celebration and sharing
  • Resources for continued learning

Teaching Tips

From experienced special education teachers across Nepal.

01
Start with student names
Have each student learn to spell their own name in both Braille and Sign Language first. Personal connection makes retention much stronger and motivates continued practice.
02
5 minutes every day
Short daily practice beats long weekly sessions. Even 5 minutes of fingerspelling warm-up at the start of class creates dramatic improvement within 2–3 weeks.
03
Pair sighted with blind students
Partner activities where sighted students learn Braille and blind students teach back creates empathy and natural cross-learning. The teaching student retains far more.
04
Make it a game
Use the quiz on SanketSiksha as a class competition on a projector. Students love the immediate feedback and the competitive element drives engagement dramatically.
05
Involve families
Send the family guide home in Nepali. When parents practice even 5 letters at dinner, student progress accelerates significantly. Home reinforcement is crucial.
06
Celebrate every milestone
Celebrate when a student masters 5 letters, then 10, then the full alphabet. Small certificates, class applause, and recognition matter enormously to young learners.
